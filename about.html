Overview

What is Digital Banking?

The term "Digital Banking" refers to banking carried out entirely online, without the need for paper documents like pay-in slips, demand drafts, or checks. It means that all banking transactions can be done online.

Although the phrase "digital banking" may be used in a variety of contexts online and off, it effectively unifies online and mobile banking services under one heading.

Online banking refers to using your computer to access banking functions and services from your bank's website. To check your balance or pay your electricity bill, log into your account. With many banks, your online banking portal gives you access to extra banking services like applying for a loan or credit card.

Using an app to access many of those same banking features on mobile devices like smartphones or tablets is called mobile banking. These apps are proprietary and typically require the same login information as your online banking portal. They are typically provided by the bank where you hold your account.

Benefits of Digital Banking

Transfer of cash

One of the most significant advantages of Digital Banking is the capability to transfer funds. There is no need to deal with the hassle of issuing checks. To send money to anyone, anywhere, at any time, all you need to do is use Digital Banking.

Obtaining bank statements

You can download bank statements for any period at any time through Digital Banking. To obtain a printout, there is no need to visit a bank branch. You can access it at any time thanks to its presence on your device.

Bills payment

Paying your bills has become so much simpler thanks to digital banking. You only need to log in to pay your bills, whether they are for phone, electricity, gas, or something else. There's also the auto-debit option, which lets you pay your bills whenever they come in.

Savings and Investments

Digital Banking has made saving and investing easier than ever. It only takes a few seconds for the bank to open a Fixed Deposit account or start a target savings account. Digital Banking can also be used to invest in other financial instruments.

Banking on the go

The internet marked the beginning of the Digital Banking revolution. Mobile phone platforms are part of Digital Banking's second phase. Digital banking has witnessed a significant growth since the introduction of smartphones.

Keeping record

Customers can now much more easily monitor their transactions thanks to digital banking. Want to know if money has been deducted from your account? Want to know about fraudulent activity on your account? You will immediately be informed.
