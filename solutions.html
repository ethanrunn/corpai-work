HALAL INVESTING / ISLAMIC BANKING

Halal investments come from Islam, specifically the Arabic word "halal." They are investments that can be made and are completely legal. Because it is both profitable and righteous, Halal investments are now practiced by everyone around the world. You can invest in halal products in four primary ways. These are:

Bonds in sukuk: A sukuk bond is similar to a traditional long-term loan bond. However, it takes a different turn because it would not provide you with a certificate that promises periodic interest-bearing payments. This is essentially due to the fact that Islam prohibits interest-driven transactions. Instead, you would own a portion of the asset and benefit from any gains it makes.

Equity funds halal: Halal equity funds only make investments in businesses that provide services that adhere to moral standards. You can't, for instance, invest in a company that makes alcohol or anything related to it.

Ijarah: This investment gives you rental income in a similar way to a lease. As a result, the way it works is that you pay a fee to transfer the rights to an asset (like a building) over time to another party. Additionally, as the asset owner, you benefit from the fixed rental charge over the particular lease term.

Murabaha: This is also referred to as cost plus financing. Since interest is against Islamic law, murabaha was created to take its place in a transaction. It works by having a buyer and a seller agree on the price and markup of an asset. As a result, the alleged interest that would have been received as a result of the transaction is compensated for by this markup.

As an investor, you can currently participate in numerous Halal investments in Nigeria.

ARBITRAGE TRADING

Despite the market's ups and downs over the years, crypto enthusiasts have found ways to make money despite the market's ups and downs. Cryptocurrencies have been around for a long time. There have been hundreds of cryptocurrency trading platforms since its inception. They all have their own features, but the prices at which you can buy and sell your assets vary between them. Crypto enthusiasts have taken advantage of this, resulting in the development of crypto arbitrage trading.

The practice of purchasing a cryptocurrency coin from one cryptocurrency exchange app and selling it at a higher price on another exchange platform is known as cryptocurrency arbitrage trading. This is possible due to the fact that various cryptocurrency trading platforms provide distinct rates that change with the market. The practice of buying an asset and selling it at a higher price in a different market is known as arbitrage trading. You can make money by buying cryptocurrencies like bitcoin, tether, and ethereum at better prices through crypto arbitrage. One of the many opportunities that cryptocurrencies offer Nigerians is arbitrage trading. However, there are associated dangers with it.

Arbitrage is a well-liked and most likely one of the oldest types of trading. Arbitrageurs are individuals who engage in arbitrage trading. Buying a product at a lower price and selling it at a higher price is the simplest form of arbitrage. You could, for instance, purchase a shoe from China at a very low price and then resell it in your city at a higher price. Arbitrage trading also involves purchasing farm produce at a discount from farmers, such as yams, to resell in larger food markets.

The practice of profiting from minute price differences between cryptocurrency exchanges is known as "crypto arbitrage trading."

ANNUITIES CONTRACT

An annuity is simply a financial agreement between a person and an insurance company that provides income for retirement or death benefits. Are you considering investing in one? Continue reading to learn about some of the advantages and drawbacks of an annuity contract. An annuity contract is a written agreement outlining each party's responsibilities in an annuity agreement between an insurance company and a customer.

The specific details of the contract, such as the annuity's structure (variable or fixed), will be included in this document. any penalties for withdrawing early; provisions for spouses and beneficiaries, such as a survivor clause and spousal coverage rate; plus more. The most important takeaways are that annuities are often financial instruments made to provide a lifetime income. An annuity contract can pass to a beneficiary upon the death of the annuitant.

There can be up to four parties involved in an annuity contract: the issuer, which is typically an insurance company, the owner of the annuity, the annuitant, and the beneficiary.


What Happens in an Annuity Contract?
An annuity contract is a legal obligation between up to four parties. They are the annuitant, the beneficiary, the owner of the annuity, and the issuer, typically an insurance company. An annuity is purchased by the owner. Annuitants are people whose life expectancy is used to figure out how much money will be paid and when benefits will start and stop. The owner and the annuitant will typically be the same person, though not always. The beneficiary is the person the owner of the annuity has chosen to receive the annuitant's death benefit.

Because it legally obligates the insurance company to provide a guaranteed periodic payment to the annuitant when the annuitant reaches retirement and requests that payments begin, an annuity contract is advantageous to the individual investor. In essence, it ensures a risk-free source of income for retirement.

An important advantage of an annuity contract is that there is no limit on how much you can contribute each year. This means that you can put a lot of money away and put off paying taxes.
